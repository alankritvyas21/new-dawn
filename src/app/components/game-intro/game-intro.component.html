New Dawn: Beyond the Fallout

Year 2147.

The world ended decades ago. Nuclear fire consumed the skies, and the surface was drowned in radiation. Only a few survived — sealed away in underground bunkers built for the end of days.

You were one of them.

Years passed in silence beneath layers of concrete and steel. The air recyclers hummed, the walls groaned, and the memories of sunlight began to fade.

When the radiation meters finally blinked green, you decided to open the hatch and face what remained.

The world outside was… not what you remembered.

Ruins stretched across the horizon, but in the distance, faint lights glimmered through the dust clouds — a sign of life.

They call it New Dawn City — built by the survivors who refused to give up. Walled and shielded, powered by scavenged tech, it's the only place where hope still flickers.

But survival comes at a cost. The air beyond the city's shield is still thick with radiation. Only the rich and powerful can afford Radi-Clear Pills, a rare miracle made by the mysterious NovaGen Industries — a company that vanished when the bombs fell.

You can't afford the pills. Not yet.

Exhausted from the journey, you find shelter in a small abandoned house just outside New Dawn's walls.

Inside:

A rusted bed frame

An empty kitchen

A broken shower leaking brown water

It's not much. But it's safe — for now.

From this house, your story begins. The wasteland still breathes, and secrets of NovaGen still linger in the ruins.
